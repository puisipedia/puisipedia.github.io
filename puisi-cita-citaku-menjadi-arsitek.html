18. Sebutkan keistimewaan cita-cita menjadi seorang arsitek dan seorang koki


Arsitek = menjadi seorang arsitek Kita bisa lebih menghargai karya Diri sendiri dan bisa bermanfaat Bagi orang yang Ingin membuat bangunan/gedung.
Koki = Kita Bisa Membuat makanan sendiri, lebih Mementingkan kesehatan Dan bisa memilih bahan Yang baik Untuk dimasakkeistimewaan dari cita-cita menjadi seorang arsitek dan seorang koki

Arsitek= bisa membuat seni bangunan yang berguna unutuk penghuni nya agar desain rumahnya menjadi bagus dilihat.

koki= bisa memasakan orang yang sedang ingin makanan. dengan rasa khas orang itu.

28. 2. Jumlah siswa kelas IV ada 40 anak. Sebanyak 18 anak bercita-cita menjadi dokter dan 12 anak bercita-cita menjadi arsitek. Berapa persen masing-masing siswa yang bercita-cita menjadi dokter dan arsitek?​


Penjelasan dengan langkah-langkah:

Dokter :

= 18/40 × 100%

= 9/20 × 100%

= 45/100 × 100%

= 45%

Arsitek :

= 12/40 × 100%

= 6/20 × 100%

= 30/100 × 100%

= 30%

%DOKTER :

= 18/40(100%)

= 0,45(100/100)

= 0,45(100)/100

= 45/100

= 45%

%ARSITEK :

= 12/40(100%)

= 0,3(100/100)

= 0,3(100)/100

= 30/100

= 30%

39. Contoh Karangan Cita Cita Menjadi Arsitek Dalam Bahasa Inggring


So the architect was my ideal ever. At first as a kid I had a hobby once playing paper dolls. Then play house-house, arrange the rooms and so on. (and always remember to make a big dressing room, because the paper doll's doll collection is plentiful). When I returned home to a grandparent in the village, where there was a field, I always played the house, built a house plan from a sand-soil compacted 3 cm high, with a door of stick. Then make the doll's life there. Just a game. Without a tendency for the future.

Approximately I was in the 5th grade when he played to a neighbor in Bandung, he had a picture table and the house was nicely arranged, my mother said, "Om Bambang is an architect." (Later on in college I knew that he was a civil engineer working in an oil company, just happen to have a drawing table)

Oh, is there a profession whose job is to build a house like a dollhouse house ?, I thought at the time. So my ideals that have been so engineer plates since childhood, began to directional. Yes, I want to be an architect. I will love this field of work.

Having goals directed to make choices in the Guidance Career during junior and senior high school easy. Always I write Architecture as first choice, with Graphic Design as second choice. Sharpen science in mathematics, physics, and chemistry, drawing and teaching economics lessons and similar IPS. Plus I'm very weak in rote, so the noneksak lessons are always jomplang compared to the exact lesson. Can 7 course have gratitude. But strangely, with the ideals of this architect, I'm not looking for more info on this profession. All I know the architect is drawing, technically designing, with a drawing table, not painting, so there's no need for picture talent. I myself can not paint at all.

So when I was at the University of Parahyangan, I was surprised that the test was a sketch image !! I was weak in drawing this also failed miserably because my sketch is very far from the expected of a prospective architect. But alhamdulillah, through the UMPTN line, I was accepted in UI architecture majors (without a test image of course) ..

The early years were spent with sketches, sketches, and sketches ... And what happened the following year? Sketches have always been an important process in working. And drawing it can be learned, as long as finding the right teacher and a strong will. Over time your sketch will be smooth, though not as smooth as the results of your friends who are clever and talented drawing.

I've been discouraged in the third year of college, for not being able to design a work. I failed in some design courses. I wanted to throw away my ideals, because anyway I prefer counting rather than drawing. I prefer to go for a walk rather than sitting in the studio. But when sitting in front of white paper, the passion returns, start again scribbling an idea. And the work was re-discovered his spirit. The important thing is concentration and intention.

Although the architect is (apparently) very tiring, because it concentrates on images and buildings continuously, but I think, if we love with this job, then we will keep trying to be better. Not just as an architect who is considered a picture by the client, but a partner is needed.

Maybe there is sometimes a desire to quit, but there is always a reason to return. When pencil in hand again, there is an idea that must be extracted.
